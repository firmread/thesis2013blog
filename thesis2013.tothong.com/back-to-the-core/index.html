Back to the core

November 30, 2012

documentation firmread Comments Off on Back to the core

We learnt one essential concept from Nick Fortugno’s Game design class yesterday. He talked about ‘stuff’ in game. It turns out when you designing game, first thing you have to figured out is the core mechanic. Then putting stuff on to that mechanic to create more complex dynamic. Without this agreeable core, stuffs won’t make any sense.

This also connected to the idea that Nick mentioned to me before about level design. The idea is, there is no right answer, only right balance. If you adjust too many variables at the same time, it is much harder to get to that balance. Just one at the time.

The relationship between core mechanic, narrative and stuff is also interesting. I asked him if narrative is also considered as stuff. He said that core and narrative are interrelated. Narrative shouldn’t be considered as stuff but it should be coming up in parallel with the core mechanic to create meaningful play experience. In relationship with stuff, narrative can be called as a point where stuff came into the core.

              

Today we discussed on this core of our game. Since our initial idea is to make an interactive narrative with a series of mini game. The weak point of this idea is that it could be lack of focus, and we might have to design too many game without making a real good game.

So we broke down the of minigames into just three different gameplay that interrelate by narrative and they are all share the same narrative goal.

This idea is also connected to the book called ‘The gift’ by Lewis Hyde, which is introduced to me in Possible World class by Melanie Crean and Huong Ngo this semester. The book have some kind of magic in the language the author used. I was so impressed that I end up ordering the book after I finished the first chapter.

You can read more detail about idea of gift economy I wrote here. But the mechanic that we thinking of now is that a parallel puzzle platformer on iPad where each player play from the opposite side and can exchange item and other stuffs to clear the puzzle.

possible narrative : both are on two planet of dream
the planet always facing the pair, therefore walking around the space cause the dream star to rotate.

For example, potion that one player got. If it been given to another player, it become more powerful. The gift cannot given back, otherwise it disappear.

Another idea is a poison cloud, when one player got poisoned. They can choose to transfer that poison to another player. The effect of the poison then lessen its effect, or change into another kind of poison. For example, poison can decrease health over time, or a reverse control, slow, jump all the time, decrease the point of view (blind/dark), transform into something else, become smaller.

There is also idea of water gun. One player search for water as a resource that another player needed as a survival tool.
