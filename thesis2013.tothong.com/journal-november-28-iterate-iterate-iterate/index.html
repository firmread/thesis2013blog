So many great stuffs going on these few days!

Yesterday we were testing one of the iPad app prototype in class, here is a video:

The prototype points out many interesting points, some are expected, some not. That’s the whole point of playtesting, isn’t it?

Everyone like the idea of negotiation on play interaction part but many interesting things emerge. Like this game was create out of narrative that should be collaborative but turns out it became competitive game. This contrast is something that we really want to develop on and looking forward to making more out of this!

 

Elevator Pitch for Barbara’s class

What it is.
Who it for.
Where it for.

Firm version:

“
We are making interactive narrative iPad app that needs two players to play.
It is an attempt to explore tablet as a medium for negotiation between the two through both interaction and narrative.
Gallery go-er, casual gamer, poets, iPad users, with portability of the platform every thinking human with an iPad, the piece could be played everywhere. However, we are also aiming to utilize AirPlay technology that would allow a piece to fit into gallery space and living room context where there is more.
“

Paul version:

“

It is a love story based experiential iPad game. It uses iPad as a experience sharing platform, allowing two players to collaboratively or separately play, to simulate everyday things in game formate for conveying the intimate feeling to both ends of the players. The players shuttle between now days and the childhoods to piece together the memory about the first love. By the end of the game, the players  have a chance to meet their first lovers, and the result depends on the differences of judgements the players decide before and the quality of the collaboration.

It is special for single persons who desire love, but it also fits couples. Single users would play this game with their potential dates for improving intimacy. Couple users would play this game as a collaborative game to achieve more intimate relationship.

“
