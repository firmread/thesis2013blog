The idea is the world inside thatThey is a dreamscape. Memories are misplaced, some are lost, and some are exaggerated. The character such as dog, birds, or rabbit can be a representation of friends inside the classroom and the boss of the game, dog’s mother; can be an angry parent of a bullyboy that represented as a dog.  Birds are like a bunch of smaller bullies these kids have to face. It is hard even just to hold on to oneself under this kind of situation but in some situation someone needs to stand up and help those are in needed. The choice would fall to the player if they would want to score on their personal safety or collaboratively protect each other.

]]> SAI-YAI สายใย http://thesis2013.tothong.com/sai-yai/ Tue, 08 Jan 2013 07:28:40 +0000 http://thesis2013.tothong.com/?p=331 The narrative concept for thesis project, an iPad game with working title ‘thatThey’, is inspired by my experience back in Thailand during my undergrad years where I went to rural northern part of Thailand with my faculty’s voluntary camp.

It was a great experience for me. I went to about seven voluntary camps throughout my undergrad years. The goal of the camp is to build a small concrete building for local school that doesn’t have enough funding from government. On the other hand, it also allows student that born and raised in big city like myself to experience life without technologies in those rural areas where the moon is still the brightest source of light at night.

I met Sarun in the last camp I attended.

Here is a translation of the blog post I wrote for Sarun a few months after the camp.

 

 

Original Thai version: http://litchirhythm.multiply.com/journal/item/8/8

SAI-YAI  (Thai for: string, relationship)

Theory is
When people start to know each other,
Develop their feeling for each other,
String of our soul,
Also tied to one another’s.

 

On the back of the truck, that carried about thirty college students back into the city from a small school in a rural uphill.
Picture of Sarun still imprint vividly in the mind of a seventh-time camper,
It was about a year and a half since his last voluntary camp.
This feeling that he once used to, once again, fresh in the air.

 

First picture is slowly packing a bag
Fold down a tent house
A small school that we live as if it was our home is now transform back to what it meant to be.
Then we take everything that we brought back, except those that meant to be given, into the truck
Just a few souvenirs in each of our hand,
And memories.
Sarun like to hold my hand
Sarun is the first camp kid that ever draws a picture of him to my notebook
Sarun is the only camp kid that asked me to jump down into a river with him

 

The day before the last day Sarun didn’t show up at the school
I was worried that we might don’t have a chance to say goodbye

 

The last morning, Sarun was back and hold my hand again
“Where were you yesterday?”

“I was helping my dad herding”

“Umm”

 

This second grade boy was much better at digging than some of the second year college student
It wouldn’t surprise me at all if his herding skill is also superior than this fifth year college student too.

 

Belongings slowly put into their containers
Trucks came in and take all students and those containers back to the town

 

The last picture was from the back of the truck with a tensile roof
It covered the truck in the way that make picture of the scenery behind appears to be so bright
Waving hands
All the camp kids were there
And indeed, Sarun too

 

The truck turn around the corner to the main street
Picture of those classrooms vanished from my eyes
A boy then ran out from that corner
And wave his hand

 

The trucks slowly run through a crooked street

 

The question is: how much distance would it takes before this this string torn apart
I imagined my soul get thinner and thinner as the truck goes on
Strings spun themselves out as the distance increase
And when it ran out of any more soul to spun, it started to stretch like a rubber
And it gets thinner and thinner
Until it torn

 

It might be right at that moment that the string that tied us together, is gone
