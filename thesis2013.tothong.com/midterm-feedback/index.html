Midterm feedback

April 8, 2013

documentation firmread Comments Off on Midterm feedback

This midterm presentation really makes me nervous since it is a first time to get a formal critique from professors that is absolutely outside the game design part of our program. On the other hand, because both Ed and Ted had never known about our project at all, it is a really good chance to get critics from someone that really ‘outside’ of the circle and have fresh eyes on the project.

In this presentation is pretty much where we concluded our thought from our common inspirations from game, interaction and art that spark our collaboration last semester to the current technical struggles that we are facing which is, ironically, also inspired us on narrative and aesthetics side of the project.

We decided on the title of the game as ‘that they’. Knowing that this name is strange and might sound grammatically wrong, and Paul even had a hard time to pronounce the word. However, the fact that it derived from Dewey’s claim on aesthetic that it related only to experiences significant to people’s memories: “that meal, that storm, that rupture of friendship.” It reflects perfectly with our struggle on coding side. As we were trying to utilize object-oriented concept from C++ to create a generic conceptual object that would eventually grows into many specific version with the method called inheritance.

Even though the playable prototype we had in the presentation was pretty much still broken and still using those placeholder geometrics, the graphical assets for the project had a lot of progress.

Ted had a really great feedback comparing our intention on making a local multiplayer game with an Ouija board. Which started as a normal board game but had eventually spiritualized in many culture.  Everyone seems like to have a control over it and at the same time have no control at all. That strange relationship that formed between the player by the game that strong enough to be interpret as if it that allows a spirit had intervene that play. It is this kind of ritualized aspect of board game where we think of it as a main inspiration: the box that contains beautiful objects and specific rules of how this little world moves. On the other side, Ouija also nicely reflects our inspiration from art world like concept of relational object or social sculpture. It feels like Ted had put a crucial puzzle piece into our list of inspirations where everything connects together.  We are absolutely grateful to have him as our critic. During spring break week we even made a few mini-game prototype that utilized the concept of hold on something together into a game already. It might easily grow into a main theme and interaction of this game. However, that still yet to decide, we would prefer leaving some rooms for playtest feedback from the class before nail down this point.

On the other hand, interactive narrative side is where we absolutely still shorts of a real work in this presentation. What we presented is more like a proposal, a break down of structure that we would like to incorporate but most real works had yet to be done. So it is really hard to criticize on anything. Ted did mentioned about how everything seems to be nice but still lacks of many crucial elements.

However, Ed gave us many good references that would push our interactive narrative side further. One of the references that he highly recommended is to read annotation version of Alice in Wonderland and Through the Looking Glass, as these two books are mirror world of each other and our game is, in many sense, also a mirror world.

I spent most of the night after the critique reading the Wikipedia page of both book. While not the best way to enjoy this great timeless fiction, but to read a full version of the book is literally jumping into a rabbit hole which we was pretty much been doing with code since this semester started. Even on Wikipedia page is just a series of stripped down facts of the book, I really enjoyed reading it. As I had saw so many movies and games that had a narrative reference to this story when I was young and had never fully understand it. Now that I know more of art history and coding skills, knowing that the author of the books, Lewis Carroll is also a mathematician and those weird concepts and dialogs in many scenes are expressing his mathematical though. I feel a strong connection to what we are doing here with our thesis as we both been making projects that had highly inspired by Zach Lieberman’s code in classes that we took with him. Those inspirations even flow over into narrative and side of the game.

I also love the story behind the novel itself, as Lewis Carroll started writing the book after he row a boat with his friend and three little daughters of his friend. It was just the kind of story non-sense story where he naturally improvise the narrative on anything he saw to make the girls enjoyed the moment. He might also have unconsciously express some mathematical concept into that story. When Alice asked him to write the book for her, he just can’t help falling in love with that fantasy world he had created and ended up expand it into a novel. This story also draws a lot of connection to our project, as Paul was started as an autobiography game and when we collaborated the theme had shifted into more fantasy, dreamscape like. With the fact that Paul’s now six-month old little daughter, Brady is so cute and growing up day by day. She is trying to speak and making weird sounds. So when I stayed over Paul’s place over spring break Paul’s small apartment always have a sound of Chinese kid’s songs sang by a family member and a murmuring Brady sounds trying to speak or sing back the same song. A girl’s mind might be the best inspiration for guys who keep thinking about the complex mathematical concept, no matter if it’s 19th century or 21st century.

Ted and Ed also had given us a numerous list of reference on game, films, and animation that we should watch or play. While those are very helpful and expands our thought as we play or watch them. I might need a bit more time to digest them to be able to elaborate on how those game might draw a connection to our game in future paper. For now, Ouija and Alice’s mirror world had strongly resonated the essence of our game. Hopefully, its finally time for this conceptual side of this project with melt away its rock candy and only diamonds would remain while we busy making it.
